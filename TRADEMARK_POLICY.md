# ObjectOS Trademark Policy

## Overview

**ObjectOS**, **Steedos**, **Huayan**, and related brand names, logos, and visual identities are trademarks owned by the ObjectOS Team. This policy explains how you may use these trademarks.

## Purpose

This trademark policy serves to:

1. Protect the integrity and reputation of the ObjectOS brand
2. Prevent confusion about the source and quality of ObjectOS software
3. Ensure that users can trust software identified as "ObjectOS"
4. Prevent misuse of our brand by competing products

## What Are Our Trademarks?

Our trademarks include, but are not limited to:

- **ObjectOS** (word mark)
- **ObjectQL** (word mark)
- **Steedos** (word mark)
- **Huayan** (word mark)
- The ObjectOS logo and visual design elements
- Any other brand names, logos, or identifiers we use to identify our products and services

## Permitted Uses

You **MAY** use our trademarks in the following ways **WITHOUT** asking for permission:

### 1. Referencing ObjectOS

- Writing articles, blog posts, or documentation that reference ObjectOS
- Accurately describing that your product is "built with ObjectOS" or "powered by ObjectOS"
- Linking to the official ObjectOS website or repository
- Using screenshots of ObjectOS in tutorials or documentation

### 2. Unmodified Distribution

- Distributing unmodified copies of ObjectOS software
- Providing ObjectOS as part of a larger software bundle (if clearly labeled)

### 3. Community Activities

- Organizing user groups, meetups, or conferences about ObjectOS (e.g., "ObjectOS User Group Shanghai")
- Creating educational content, tutorials, or training materials
- Contributing to ObjectOS development and documentation

**IMPORTANT:** In all permitted uses, you must:
- Make it clear that ObjectOS is a trademark of the ObjectOS Team
- Not imply official endorsement or sponsorship unless you have written permission
- Not use our trademarks in a misleading or disparaging manner

## Prohibited Uses

You **MUST NOT** use our trademarks in the following ways:

### 1. Competing Products

❌ **You cannot use ObjectOS trademarks for any competing product or service.**

If you create a modified or forked version of ObjectOS that competes with ObjectOS itself (such as a general-purpose low-code backend platform, BaaS engine, or database middleware), you **MUST**:

- **Remove ALL ObjectOS branding** including:
  - Brand names (ObjectOS, Steedos, Huayan, ObjectQL)
  - Logos and visual design elements
  - Any text or code comments that reference our trademarks
- **Choose a completely different name** for your product
- **NOT claim or imply** that your product is ObjectOS or an official version of ObjectOS

### 2. Misleading or Confusing Use

❌ You cannot:
- Use our trademarks in a way that suggests your product is an official ObjectOS product
- Use our trademarks in domain names (e.g., `objectos-alternative.com`) without permission
- Register company names, trademarks, or domain names that include our trademarks
- Use our trademarks in a way that disparages ObjectOS or harms our reputation

### 3. Merchandising

❌ You cannot:
- Sell merchandise (t-shirts, stickers, etc.) featuring our trademarks without permission
- Use our trademarks for commercial purposes unrelated to ObjectOS software

## Modified Versions and Forks

### Non-Competing Forks

If you create a modified version that **does NOT compete** with ObjectOS (for example, a specialized industry-specific solution):

✅ You **MAY** include language like:
- "Based on ObjectOS"
- "Forked from ObjectOS"
- "Built using ObjectOS technology"

✅ You **MUST**:
- Make it clear your product is a derivative work and not the official ObjectOS
- Use your own product name and branding as the primary identifier
- Maintain all copyright and license notices

### Competing Forks

If you create a competing product (as defined in our license):

❌ You **MUST**:
- Remove ALL ObjectOS trademarks and branding
- NOT reference ObjectOS in your product name
- NOT market your product as being related to ObjectOS

## Examples

### ✅ Allowed Examples

1. **Developer A builds a CRM system:**
   - "Built with ObjectOS" on their website ✅
   - Uses unmodified ObjectOS as their backend ✅

2. **Enterprise B creates internal tools:**
   - Runs ObjectOS as their data platform internally ✅
   - Creates company-specific extensions ✅

3. **Educator C creates a course:**
   - "Learn ObjectOS Development" course title ✅
   - Uses ObjectOS screenshots in teaching materials ✅

### ❌ Prohibited Examples

1. **Competitor C forks ObjectOS:**
   - Renames it to "SuperQL" but keeps ObjectOS logos in the code ❌
   - Markets "SuperQL" as a "better ObjectOS" ❌
   - Uses domain name `superql-objectos.com` ❌

2. **Company D creates a competing product:**
   - Launches "ObjectOS Pro" as a competing BaaS platform ❌
   - Uses "Powered by ObjectOS" when it's a completely different codebase ❌
